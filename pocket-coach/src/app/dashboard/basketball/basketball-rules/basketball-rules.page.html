Basketball Rules Scoring
  • When a team scores through a hoop, they receive two points and the ball goes to the other team
  • If the score is made beyond the three-point arc, the basket is worth three points
Fouls
  • A foul is any type of illegal physical contact that includes hitting, pushing, or holding
  • When a shooter is fouled, they are award free throws depending on where they were fouled
    • Each free throw is worth one point
    • If the shooter makes the basket as they were being fouled, they are awarded the bucket and a free throw
    • Two free throws if the shooter was fouled on a two point attempt
    • Three free throws if the shooter was attempting a three
  • If a player was fouled but they were not shooting, the team that was fouled would inbound the ball and the shot clock would reset

Violations
  • Any of these violations will result in a turnover, which is when you give the other team the ball
    • Walking or Travelling: Taking more than two steps without dribbling the ball. You may also not move your pivot foot once you stopped dribbling
    • Carrying or Palming: When you dribble the ball too far to the side of the ball
    • Double Dribble: Dribbling the ball with both hands on the ball or when you dribble, stop dribbling, then dribble again
    • Backcourt Violation: Once your team has passed the half court line, you guys are not allowed to go back across the line unless unless it was deflected by the other team
    • Time Restrictions: A team has 5 seconds to inbound the ball. They also have 24 seconds to attempt to score.
    • Out of Bounds: If the offensive team was the last ones to touch the ball before going out of bounds, the ball would go to the other team
