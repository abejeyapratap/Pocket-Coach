Soccer Rules Basic Rules
  • A match consists of two 45 minutes halves with a 15 minute rest period in between
  • The field must be made of either natural or artificial grass.
  • The size of the pitch must be within 100-130 yards long and 50-100 yards wide
  • Maximum of 11 players (goalkeeper included), and a minimum of seven players is needed to constitute a match
  • Each team is only allowed to make a maximum of 3 substitutions. If a team uses all three substitutions and a player has to leave the field for injury, the team will be forced to continue the game without having a replacement for the player
  • If the game is tied after the allotted 90 minutes, the match will continue in overtime: overtime consists of 30 minutes, with two 15 minute halves
  • If the game is still tied after overtime, a penalty shootout will determine the winner
  • In order for a goal to be counted, the whole ball must cross the goal line
  • For fouls committed by a player, they could receive either a yellow or red card depending on the severity of the foul and the referee’s discretion.
    • Fouls include pushing, tripping, and touching the ball
  • If the ball goes out of an opponent in either of the side lines then it is given as a throw in.
    • Throw in rules:
      • Ball must be thrown from behind the head
      • Feet must be behind the line (one foot can be touching the line)
      • A ball cannot be scored directly from a throw in
        • If it enters the goal, a goal kick is awarded
  • If the ball goes out of play off an attacking player on the baseline then it is given as a goal kick
  • If a foul occur in the penalty area, the fouled team is awarded a penalty kick
 Advanced Rules
  • Offside Rule
    • The offensive player is offside if they are nearer to the opponent’s goal line than both the second and last opponent and the soccer ball
